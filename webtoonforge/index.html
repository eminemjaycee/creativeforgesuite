Process

From feature script to
full webtoon series

Three steps between your finished screenplay and a deliverable your art team can work from immediately.

📄

Paste or upload your script

Drop in any screenplay in Fountain format — full features, pilots, or shorts. WebtoonForge reads scene headings, action, and dialogue automatically.

✂️

Auto-split into episodes

The engine analyzes narrative beats and pacing, splitting your script into webtoon episodes of 24–48 pages each. You control episode length.

🎨

Export panel scripts

Every page gets panel breakdowns with shot types, visual descriptions, dialogue bubble placement, sound FX, and caption notes — ready to hand to an artist.

📤

Export & publish

Download each episode as a formatted .txt panel script or copy directly to your workflow. Upload to Webtoon, Tapas, or your art team's pipeline.

What you get

Everything your art team needs

No guesswork. No back-and-forth. Every panel described with enough precision to draw from directly.

Panel Breakdown

Shot-level visual descriptions

Each panel gets a shot type (OTS, WIDE, CLOSE, POV), character positions, expressions, and scene environment — exactly what an artist needs to lay out the page.

Dialogue

Bubble placement guidance

Dialogue is mapped to specific panels with bubble placement notes — left, right, overlapping — so your letterer knows exactly where text lands on the page.

Sound & Captions

SFX and narration boxes

Action scenes get sound effect suggestions (CRACK, WHOOSH, SLAM). Narrator boxes from action lines are converted to webtoon caption format automatically.

Episode Structure

Narrative-aware pacing

Episode splits happen at natural story beats — act turns, cliffhangers, scene transitions — not arbitrary page counts. Your story reads the way you intended.

Export

Plain text, no lock-in

Output is clean, readable plain text your art team can open in anything. No proprietary formats, no subscriptions required to read what you exported.

Format

Fountain & FDX support

Works with standard Fountain (.fountain) and Final Draft XML (.fdx) formats. If your software can export either, WebtoonForge can read it.
